Web Sustainability

What can I do as a designer?

Digital Carbon Footprint

Definition: CO₂ emissions from the production, use and data transfer of digital devices

Color

Video, image, and animation-heavy websites are often those which can have significant sustainability costs not only in terms of carbon intensity but also in draining batteries. Optimizing such assets as much as possible is critical for a sustainable product or service.

The following are best practices to reduce digital carbon footprints.

* Reconsider the need for media: Assess the need for imagery or videos and include only when they add visitor value. Ban non-informative media, such as background media and autoplaying functionality.

* Optimize media: If you have chosen to include media, compress the file size if possible, select the appropriate format, and avoid embedded player plugins.

* Include labels and choice: Increase visitor awareness and control by informing them of the length, format, and weight of the media. Allow media deactivation, and provide alternative resolutions and formats.

* Dither images: Dithering can make images less resource-intensive by a factor of ten or more while retaining the same image size.

Font

Loading embedded fonts has a carbon impact (as the physical rendering of non-system typefaces graphically onto every element of the DOM will have an energy commitment). The following are best practices to reduce digital carbon footprints.

* Use default typefaces: Use standard system-level (web-safe / pre-installed) fonts as much as possible.

* Optimize fonts: Ensure the number of fonts, and the variants within typefaces (such as weight and characters) are limited within a project.
